## THE RUSSELL FAMILY FOUNDATION — FULL PROFILE
- **Location:** Gig Harbor, WA (45 min from Seattle — local funder)
- **Website:** trff.org
- **Assets:** ~$100 million
- **Focus:** Catalytic climate finance — grants to organizations that can unlock larger capital flows for climate solutions
- **Grant to Maritime Blue:** $50,000 (January 2024) — Blue Ventures programs and operations
- **Why they gave:** Maritime Blue's Blue Economy accelerator model aligns with TRFF's thesis of catalytic climate investment (small grants that unlock larger ecosystem impact)
- **Relationship type:** Foundation grant — requires stewardship reports, impact updates, renewal cultivation

### Leadership
- **CEO:** Kathleen Simpson (since 2020)
- **Phone:** 253-858-5050
- **Address:** 3025 Harborview Dr, Gig Harbor, WA 98335

### Grant Process
- Reports submitted via Grant Portal ~13 months after grant approval
- Extensions available via email request
- Foundation may reach out to grantees for photos/stories for their communications

### Four Pillars of Catalytic Climate Finance Program
1. Accelerate GHG emissions reduction
2. Early-stage funding for grassroots movements
3. Collaboration across climate sector
4. Equity in the transition (protecting most impacted communities)

### 2030 Targets
- Carbon emissions intensity: max 28 metric tons CO₂ per $1M invested
- Min 75% of assets invested in climate solutions
- Member of UN Net Zero Asset Owners Alliance (NZAOA)

### What They Want in Stewardship Reports
- **Stories + metrics** — both narrative and numbers
- Emissions reduction potential and scalability
- Community/equity impact
- Open sharing of progress AND challenges
- Photos, videos, and content they can spotlight

### Tone & Language to Use
- "Partnership," "collaboration," "collective action"
- "Meeting the moment" — acknowledge urgency
- Equity and belonging language
- Solution-oriented, forward-looking
- Professional but accessible — not overly academic

### Come funziona il rapporto TRFF → Maritime Blue
TRFF è una **family foundation privata** con una logica di grant-making specifica: **"catalytic climate finance"**. Non finanziano progetti isolati — finanziano organizzazioni che fungono da **moltiplicatori di capitale**. Il ragionamento: ogni $1 investito in Maritime Blue può attrarne $10+ da investitori privati tramite il loro acceleratore.

**Il ciclo del rapporto donor-nonprofit:**
1. TRFF eroga il grant → Maritime Blue riceve i fondi
2. Il Development Manager **steward** il rapporto: ringraziamento formale, aggiornamenti sull'impatto, report periodici
3. L'obiettivo è costruire una **relazione continuativa** che porti al rinnovo del grant o a un aumento

**Per il stewardship:** la chiave è dimostrare che il loro $50K ha prodotto impatto moltiplicato — quante startup supportate, quanti posti di lavoro creati, quanti investimenti attratti nel settore. Questo è esattamente il linguaggio che TRFF vuole sentire.
